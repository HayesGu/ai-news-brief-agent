# Synthetic Article: Algorithmic Mediation and Workplace Voice

This synthetic research article examines how AI scheduling systems may affect worker voice in
platform-mediated labor markets. The study imagines interviews with delivery workers and a small
simulation of shift allocation rules.

The central claim is that optimization systems can reduce visible conflict while increasing hidden
coordination costs for workers. Workers adapt by sharing informal strategies, but these strategies
are unevenly distributed across language groups and tenure.

The proposed method combines qualitative coding with network analysis of peer-support ties. The
article argues that computational social science can help connect platform governance, labor
inequality, and institutional accountability when data limitations are made explicit.
